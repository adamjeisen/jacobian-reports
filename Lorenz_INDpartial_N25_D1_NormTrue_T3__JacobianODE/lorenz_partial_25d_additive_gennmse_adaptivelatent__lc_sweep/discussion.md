**Success criteria.** (1) *Leading Lyapunov exponent > 0*: **Pass.** The best run (i70i2ohj, LC = 1e-6) has lambda_1 = +0.130 (full-trajectory) and +0.199 (batch windowed), both positive, confirming recovered chaos. In fact, 18 of 22 runs show lambda_1 > 0; only the two LC = 10 runs (lambda_1 = -1.72) and one LC = 1e-6 run (yzwnzk9t, lambda_1 = -0.04) have non-positive leading exponents. (2) *Predicted spectrum within ~30% of empirical*: **Fail.** Against the empirical spectrum [+0.271, -0.066, -13.87], the best run's full-trajectory estimates are [+0.123, -0.909, -13.20]. lambda_3 is within 5%, but lambda_1 is underestimated by 55% and lambda_2 is off by an order of magnitude (-0.91 vs -0.07). This pattern holds broadly: the per_run_lyapunov_relerr plot shows lambda_2 relative error exceeding 1000% for nearly every run, indicating a systematic difficulty resolving the near-zero second exponent. (3) *LPL stays numerically well-scaled*: **Partial.** No epoch-level LPL curves are available in this analysis to verify directly. However, the best runs at LC in [1e-6, 1e-4] all trained to the full 200 epochs without early stopping, producing trajectory losses in the 6.4e-5 to 7.2e-5 range with R^2 > 0.9999, suggesting the adaptive-latent denominator did not cause blow-up or vanishing. The one clear failure (tkb16u0a, LC = 0.1) stopped after 1 epoch with MASE = 6.9, but its three sibling LC = 0.1 runs trained normally, pointing to an initialization pathology rather than a systematic LPL scaling issue. (4) *Match or beat the fixed-gennMSE baseline*: **Cannot evaluate** — no baseline numbers are present in this analysis directory.

**LC landscape.** The sweep reveals a broad, flat optimum across LC weights from 0 to 1e-3. The best trajectory loss (6.4e-5) and MASE (0.048) occur at LC = 1e-6, but LC = 1e-5 and 1e-4 are nearly indistinguishable (traj loss 6.5e-5 to 6.9e-5, MASE 0.050 to 0.058). Performance degrades modestly at LC = 0.01 (traj loss ~1.9e-4, MASE 0.13) and LC = 1.0 (traj loss 1.2e-4, MASE 0.11), and sharply at LC = 10 (traj loss 3.7e-3, MASE 0.93). The LC = 0 runs without any loop closure still achieve traj loss 7.1e-5, only marginally worse than the best, suggesting the adaptive-latent gennMSE already provides strong training signal on its own and loop closure offers diminishing returns here. The Pareto frontier confirms this: progressing from LC = 10 down to LC = 1e-6 trades monotonically decreasing LC loss for monotonically decreasing trajectory loss.

**Lyapunov analysis.** The per_run_lyapunov_vs_true figure shows that well-trained runs (LC in [0, 0.1]) consistently recover the qualitative shape of the true spectrum — a positive lambda_1, a near-zero lambda_2, and a strongly negative lambda_3 — but systematically compress the top two exponents toward each other. The lyapunov_spectrum_mse_vs_val_loss plot confirms a clear monotonic relationship: runs with lower trajectory val loss also achieve lower spectrum MSE, with the best cluster (traj loss < 1e-4) reaching spectrum MSE of 0.17-0.52. The LC = 10 and early-stopped tkb16u0a runs are outliers with spectrum MSE > 25, reflecting dynamics that have collapsed to a stable fixed point or failed to train. Kaplan-Yorke dimension for the best run is 1.14, below the empirical value implied by the true spectrum (~2.01), consistent with the compressed lambda_1 and lambda_2 estimates.

**Anomalies and hypothesis.** One LC = 0.1 run (tkb16u0a) is a clear outlier that early-stopped at epoch 0 with MASE 6.9 and R^2 = 0.88 — likely an unlucky initialization since the other three LC = 0.1 runs are well-behaved. Several LC values have exact duplicate run pairs (identical metrics), presumably from duplicated sweep entries; these do not affect conclusions. The hypothesis is **partially supported**: the adaptive-latent mode produces stable training across a wide LC range with no evidence of LPL blow-up, and the best runs achieve excellent trajectory prediction (R^2 > 0.9999, MASE < 0.05). However, the Lyapunov spectrum recovery criterion is not met, and without the fixed-denominator baseline in this analysis we cannot confirm whether adaptive-latent actually improved the LC landscape shape.
